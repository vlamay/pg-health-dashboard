# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or technical ability.

We expect all contributors, maintainers, and community members to be respectful and constructive in all interactions within our community.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior Includes

- Being kind and constructive in feedback
- Being respectful of differing opinions
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Attributing work and ideas to others appropriately
- Providing helpful guidance to newcomers

### Unacceptable Behavior Includes

- Harassment or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body, age, race, or religion
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or doxxing
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Violent threats or violent language
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors
- Sustained disruption of community spaces (forums, comments, pull requests, etc.)
- Spam or commercial promotion without community consensus

---

## Enforcement Expectations

Community leaders will:

1. **Clarify** standards of acceptable behavior and consequences
2. **Address** violations promptly and fairly
3. **Investigate** reports of violations
4. **Take action** proportionate to the severity
5. **Maintain** the privacy and security of reporters

### Levels of Response

**1. Warning**
- First minor violations
- Respectful private communication
- Clear explanation of the issue
- Request for behavioral change

**2. Temporary Suspension**
- Continued violations or serious first offense
- Suspension from contributing for specified period
- Opportunity for reflection and improvement

**3. Permanent Ban**
- Severe violations
- Repeated violations after warnings
- Threats or harassment
- Harmful behavior impacting community

---

## Reporting Process

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior:

### How to Report

1. **Direct Contact** (Preferred for minor issues)
   - Message the relevant project maintainer privately
   - Include specific details, times, and context

2. **Formal Report** (For serious issues)
   - Email: [conduct-report@example.com](mailto:conduct-report@example.com)
   - Include:
     - Your name and contact info
     - Description of the incident
     - People involved (if comfortable sharing)
     - How the incident affected you
     - Any witnesses

3. **Anonymous Report** (For sensitive issues)
   - Use form at [example.com/conduct-report](https://example.com/conduct-report)
   - Provide context without identifying yourself if needed

### Response Timeline

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Investigation**: 7-14 days
- **Resolution**: Within 30 days when possible
- **Follow-up**: Closure communication

### Confidentiality

- Reports are confidential
- Details shared only with those investigating
- Reporter safety is prioritized
- No retaliation permitted

## Examples

### ✅ Constructive Examples

**Giving feedback on a pull request:**
> "Great work on this feature! I think the polling interval could be optimized. Have you considered using the adaptive strategy from the architecture doc? I'd be happy to discuss if you'd like."

**Discussing design disagreements:**
> "I see this differently, and here's why. The animation timing might affect performance in high-latency environments. Could we test both approaches and measure the impact?"

**Welcoming newcomers:**
> "Welcome! Glad to have you contributing. If you're new to the codebase, start with the dev setup guide. Feel free to ask questions in the issues—we're here to help!"

**Addressing a mistake:**
> "I noticed we missed this edge case in the error handling. Let's add a test for it and make sure it's covered. No worries—these things happen!"

---

## Non-Examples

### ❌ Unacceptable Examples

**Dismissive feedback:**
> "This code is garbage. Go learn how to code."

**Gatekeeping:**
> "You clearly don't understand X. You shouldn't contribute to real projects."

**Personal attacks:**
> "Your background in biology means you can't understand backend development."

**Harassment:**
> "I'm going to find out where you live and teach you a lesson."

## Community Health

### Building a Healthy Community

- **Assume good intent** - Most conflicts are unintentional
- **Communicate clearly** - Tone is lost in text; be explicit
- **Listen actively** - Seek to understand before responding
- **Admit mistakes** - Model the behavior you want to see
- **Celebrate others** - Give credit and recognition generously
- **Mentor newcomers** - Help others succeed
- **Address issues promptly** - Don't let toxicity fester

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Repeated negative comments toward individuals or groups
- Unwelcoming attitudes toward newcomers
- Taking personal disagreements outside the project
- Using the project for harassment campaigns
- Spreading rumors or misinformation

## Questions?

If you're unsure whether something violates this Code of Conduct, ask:

- Comment in the relevant issue or PR
- Message a maintainer privately
- Check the "Examples" section above
- Email conduct-report@example.com

**Default**: When in doubt, err on the side of kindness and inclusivity.
